Delivery & Defense

Project validation, strategic justification, and lessons learned.

1. Technical Feasibility Validation

Our Proof of Concept (PoC) successfully demonstrated the core value proposition:

  • Sensor Integration: The Raspberry Pi 4 + IMX219 setup successfully captured clear images inside a test fridge environment (4Â°C).
  • AI Performance: The YOLOv8 model achieved an 88% mAP (mean Average Precision) on our custom dataset of 50 common grocery items.
  • Connectivity: Real-time inventory updates were pushed to the mobile app via MQTT with < 2 seconds latency.
Conclusion: The technology is ready for industrial refinement.

2. Budget Strategy Justification

We prioritized CAPEX on Tooling ($40k) early in the process. Why?

While 3D printing is cheaper for prototypes, it does not simulate the structural integrity of the final product. Investing in soft-steel molds allows us to produce 100+ units for beta testing that are physically identical to the mass-produced version. This reduces the risk of "design-for-manufacturing" failures later.

OPEX Efficiency: We utilized contract workers for specialized tasks (Industrial Design) rather than full-time hires, saving approximately $15,000 in annual overhead.

3. Handling Pitfalls & Unforeseen Events

Pitfall: Sensor Fogging

Issue: During Week 6 testing, opening the fridge door caused immediate condensation on the camera lens, rendering the AI blind.

Resolution: We pivoted the design to include a small heating element ring around the lens (borrowed from automotive camera designs) and applied a hydrophobic coating.

Pitfall: Supply Chain Delay

Issue: Raspberry Pi modules went out of stock globally in Week 3.

Resolution: We activated our risk contingency budget to purchase units from a secondary market at a premium (+30% cost) to keep the timeline on track, proving the value of our risk management fund.

4. Agile Hardware Methodology

We adopted an Agile Hardware approach, synchronizing hardware and software development in 2-week sprints.

  • Recursive Planning: Our Gantt chart was a "living document". For example, when the sensor fogging issue arose in Sprint 3, we immediately inserted a "Hardware Revision" sprint without derailing the software team.
  • Cross-Functional Sync: Hardware schematics and AI model updates were reviewed together every sprint review, ensuring the camera placement matched the AI's field-of-view requirements.
