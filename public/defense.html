Budget Strategy & ROI

CAPEX vs OPEX

We prioritized a high initial CAPEX (€98.5k) to reduce long-term unit costs.

  • Molds (€57k): Investing in steel molds guarantees 500k cycles, reducing casing cost by 60% vs soft tooling.
  • Test Bench (€8.5k): Automating camera calibration saves €15/unit in manual labor.

Cost Structure

Our BOM cost of €140 allows for a retail price of €299 with a healthy margin, undercutting competitors by 30%.

"Break-even point is reached at 2,500 units sold, expected within Month 4 of launch."

Market Risk Mitigation

Addressing Adoption Barriers

The "Smart Fridge" market suffers from a perception of high cost and low utility. Our strategy addresses this head-on:

Risk: "It's too expensive"

Solution: Hardware-as-a-Service model option. €49 upfront + €9.99/mo subscription including auto-replenishment.

Risk: "It's a gimmick"

Solution: Focus on "Zero Waste" ROI. The app calculates money saved by not throwing away food, gamifying the experience.

Agile Planning & Adaptation

Pivot: Supply Chain Crisis

When the STM32 shortage hit (Risk #2), we didn't just wait.

  • Action: Redesigned PCB with dual-footprint for GigaDevice alternative.
  • Impact: 2 weeks delay in design (absorbed by slack), but secured production start.

Iterative Quality

We added a specific "Quality Assurance Plan" task and a dedicated risk for manufacturing defects after realizing our initial plan was too optimistic about yield.

"Planning is living document. We updated the Gantt 4 times during the conception phase."
